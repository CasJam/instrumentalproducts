## System Requirements

In my case, I identified a few &#8220;System Requirements&#8221; for Restaurant Engine&#8217;s sales system:

  * **Education-based.** Â We already excel at education-based content marketing. Â This must work hand-in-hand with our sales system.
  * **Remove myself.**Â  Although I have become quite comfortable with sales calls over the past year, I recognize it&#8217;s not my strength. Â I need to spend my time on other things. Â So I brought in a new person to take over most of these sales calls. Â A key requirement of this system is it must allow for easy collaborate with my sales team.
  * **Remote.** Â My team is 100% remote. Â That means our systems must play nicely with a remote working environment.
  * **Efficient.** Â I want to spend our time wisely. Â That means we must spend all of our time communicating with customers and practically no time fumbling around our CRM, doing manual data entry, etc. Â All of the &#8220;mechanics&#8221; must be automated.

  * **Measurable.** Â We must have ways to measure how we&#8217;re doing from month-to-month.

### Integrating Gravity Forms & Trello using Zapier

HelpForWP sellsÂ [a nifty plugin](http://helpforwp.com/downloads/gravity-forms-to-trello/) for integrating Gravity Forms & Trello. Â However, I ended up using [Zapier](http://zapier.com/) instead. Zapier is a tool for integrating (almost) any app with any other app. Â Both Gravity Forms and Trello have &#8220;Zaps&#8221; available for use within Zapier, which basically means they&#8217;ve made their API&#8217;s available to connect with other apps using Zapier&#8217;s easy interface.

Look at the 2nd screenshot above, showing the detail of one card (for one lead) in Trello. Â Everything you see there has been _automatically_ created using the Gravity Forms/Trello integration via Zapier. Â Here&#8217;s what happens:

  1. A visitor fills out one of our Gravity Forms on our site.
  2. A new card is created in Trello, placed in the list that this particular Gravity Form is associated with (each list is a different lead source). Â The person&#8217;s name and Restaurant Name become title of the card.
  3. The customer&#8217;s info, which they had entered into the Gravity Form, is populated in the description area of the Trello Card. Â Using Markdown, I formatted this so that it&#8217;s easy to read.
  4. I also told Zapier to add a Checklist to every new card, which is a procedure that my sales team follows for every new lead.
  5. Finally, I told Zapier to set a &#8220;Due Date&#8221; for every new card, 72 hours after the creation date. Â This gives us 3 days to contact the lead before Trello starts telling us we&#8217;re falling behind.

With a steady stream of new leads, complete with their contact info and restaurant info, we&#8217;re ready to start making calls and reaching out.
